Tracking Your Progress

Tools, methods, and templates for measuring progress

Why Track?

What gets measured gets managed. Tracking your key health metrics allows you to:

  • Identify trends and patterns over time
  • Validate that your interventions are working
  • Catch potential health issues early
  • Stay motivated by seeing objective progress
  • Make data-driven adjustments to your approach

How to Track Each Metric

Strength

Tools:

  • Hand dynamometer for grip strength (~$25-50)
  • Workout log or app (Strong, Google Sheets, notebook)
  • Video recording for form checks

Frequency:

  • Log every workout (exercises, sets, reps, weight)
  • Test grip strength monthly
  • Test 1RM or rep maxes every 4-8 weeks

VO2 Max

Tools:

  • Wearable devices (Apple Watch, Garmin, Whoop) for estimates
  • Laboratory VO2 max test (gold standard)
  • Field tests (Cooper test, beep test)

Frequency:

  • Wearable estimates: continuous
  • Field tests: every 4-8 weeks
  • Lab testing: annually or bi-annually

LDL Cholesterol

Tools:

  • Standard lipid panel (physician order or direct lab)
  • Advanced lipid testing (LDL-P, ApoB) for detailed analysis

Frequency:

  • Annual testing minimum
  • Every 3-6 months if optimizing or on medication
  • Fasting not required for standard panels

Blood Pressure

Tools:

  • Home blood pressure monitor (automatic cuff, ~$30-100)
  • Validated devices recommended by medical associations

Frequency:

  • Daily if managing hypertension
  • Weekly if monitoring for trends
  • Same time each day, rested, proper technique

Proper Technique:

  • Sit quietly for 5 minutes before measurement
  • Feet flat on floor, back supported
  • Arm at heart level, cuff on bare skin
  • Take 2-3 readings, average them

Body Fat Percentage

Tools:

  • DEXA scan (most accurate, ~$50-150 per scan)
  • Bioelectrical impedance scale (convenient, less accurate)
  • Skinfold calipers (requires practice, ~$10-30)
  • Progress photos (qualitative but useful)

Frequency:

  • DEXA: every 3-6 months
  • Scale/calipers: weekly (same conditions each time)
  • Photos: bi-weekly (same lighting, time, angles)

Creating Your Tracking System

An effective tracking system should be:

  • Consistent: Measure under the same conditions
  • Sustainable: Simple enough to maintain long-term
  • Actionable: Data you can actually use to make decisions
  • Comprehensive: Cover all five key metrics

Sample Tracking Schedule

Daily:

  • Blood pressure (if managing hypertension)
  • Workout logging (on training days)

Weekly:

  • Body weight and body fat % (same day/time)
  • Blood pressure check (if monitoring)

Monthly:

  • Grip strength test
  • Progress photos
  • Review trends and adjust approach

Quarterly:

  • DEXA scan or detailed body composition
  • Lipid panel (LDL cholesterol)
  • VO2 max field test

Annually:

  • Comprehensive health screening
  • Advanced lipid testing
  • Reassess goals and protocols
