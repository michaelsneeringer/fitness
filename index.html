Our Philosophy

  • Five metrics, not fifty: Strength, VO2 max, body fat, LDL cholesterol, and blood pressure -- these are the numbers that actually predict how long and how well you'll live
  • Minimum effective dose: One hour of intense training and 90 minutes of easy cardio per week -- because a program you'll actually follow beats a perfect program you won't
  • Research over rhetoric: Every recommendation on this site is grounded in peer-reviewed science, not influencer wisdom or supplement marketing
  • Sustainability over speed: No crash diets, no extreme protocols -- just a straightforward approach designed to work for years, not weeks
  • Measure what matters: Track your progress with real data so you can see what's working, catch what isn't, and make adjustments based on evidence instead of feelings

What You Can Expect

So you're still reading. Good. Before you dive in, here's what this program actually looks like in practice -- because you deserve to know what you might be signing up for.

The training commitment is smaller than you think. You'll spend about one hour per week on intense exercise -- strength training and high-intensity cardio -- and roughly 90 minutes per week on low-intensity exercise like walking on an incline or easy cycling. That's it. No two-a-days, no six-day splits, no living at the gym.

The diet doesn't require you to count calories, eliminate food groups, or say goodbye to your favorite foods. You won't be eating boiled chicken and broccoli out of tupperware, unless you like that sort of thing. You'll learn a sustainable approach that works with your life, not against it.

Results will come slowly. That's not a warning -- it's a feature. You won't see dramatic changes in a week, but you will see consistent, measurable progress month after month. Small gains will compound. Six months from now, the numbers will tell a story that's hard to argue with.

Getting Started

A simple, three-step approach to improving your health -- no fads, no gimmicks, just science.

1

Identify the Metrics That Matter

Learn which five health metrics are backed by decades of research as the strongest predictors of longevity and quality of life. These aren't vanity numbers or arbitrary benchmarks -- they're the biomarkers that consistently show up in large-scale studies on healthspan and mortality. Understanding what to measure is the first step toward knowing where you actually stand. Once you have that baseline, everything else has a clear direction.

2

Follow a Simple, Evidence-Based Plan

Each metric has a straightforward, science-backed protocol to improve it. No complicated programs, no expensive supplements -- just the methods that the research shows actually move the needle. The plans are designed to fit into a normal life, not take it over. You'll know exactly what to do, why it works, and how little time it actually requires.

3

Track Your Progress

Use practical tools and templates to measure your progress over time. Consistent tracking turns vague feelings into concrete data, so you always know whether your approach is working. You'll have a clear schedule for when to test each metric and simple methods to record the results. What gets measured gets managed -- and what gets managed gets better.
