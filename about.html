Getting Started

Our evidence-based approach to fitness and health

Our Philosophy

The fitness industry is saturated with misinformation, fad diets, and quick-fix solutions. Every week there's a new superfood, miracle supplement, or diet trend promising to revolutionize your health.

This site exists to cut through the noise and focus on what the scientific evidence actually shows matters for long-term health and longevity.

We believe in:

  • Evidence over anecdote: Peer-reviewed research trumps testimonials
  • Fundamentals over fads: The basics work better than tricks and hacks
  • Metrics over aesthetics: Health markers predict longevity better than appearance
  • Honesty over hype: No magic pills, no shortcuts, no nonsense
  • Accessibility over complexity: Simple, actionable information anyone can use

Why These Five Metrics?

After reviewing decades of research on health, longevity, and disease prevention, five metrics consistently emerge as the most predictive of long-term health outcomes:

  1. Strength - Predicts all-cause mortality, maintains independence, protects against metabolic disease
  2. VO2 Max - The gold standard for cardiovascular fitness and mortality risk
  3. LDL Cholesterol - The primary driver of atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease
  4. Blood Pressure - A major risk factor for heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease
  5. Body Fat Percentage - More relevant than body weight for metabolic health and disease risk

These aren't the only health metrics that matter, but they represent the most impactful areas where lifestyle interventions can make measurable differences in health span and lifespan.

Our Commitment to Science

Every claim on this site is backed by peer-reviewed research. We prioritize:

  • Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
  • Randomized controlled trials
  • Large-scale observational studies

We provide direct citations and links to original research so you can verify the information yourself. We update content as new research emerges and correct any errors promptly.
